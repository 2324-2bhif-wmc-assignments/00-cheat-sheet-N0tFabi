Cheat Sheet Reading Assignments Quizzes / Tests

Html - Head Element

Title

The Title is defined by the <title> tag. It is displayed in the browser tab.
Example:
<title>My Website</title>

MetaTags

Metadatas are informations about the HTML Document.
Examples:
<meta charset="UTF-8">
<meta name="description" content="My Website's Description">
<meta name="author" content="Author's name">

Links and Scripts

Links and Scripts are references to styles or scripts or other assets required for the webpages's functionality.
Example:
<link rel="stylesheet" href="styles.css">
<script src="script.js"></script>

Html - Body Element

Headings

Headings are used to structure the content of a webpage. There are 6 different headings, <h1> being the most important and <h6> being the least important.
Example:
<h1>Heading 1</h1>
<h2>Heading 2</h2>
<h3>Heading 3</h3>
<h4>Heading 4</h4>
<h5>Heading 5</h5>
<h6>Heading 6</h6>

Paragraphs (<p>)

Paragraphs are used to display text in a block format. They are commonly used for structuring content and separating text.

Example:

<p>This is a paragraph.</p>

Links (<a>)

Links are used to connect to other webpages or resources. They are fundamental for navigation and connecting content across the web.

Example:

<a href="https://www.google.com">Go to Google</a>

Headings (<h1> to <h6>)

Headings are used to define titles or subtitles for sections of content. They vary in size from h1 (largest) to h6 (smallest).

Example:

<h1>Main Heading</h1>
<h2>Subheading</h2>
<h3>Another Subheading</h3>

Images (<img>)

Images are used to display visual content on a webpage. They can be photos, illustrations, icons, etc., and are specified using the src attribute.

Example:

<img src="image.jpg" alt="Description of the image">

Lists (Ordered & Unordered)

Lists are used to organize content in a structured manner. They can be ordered (<ol>) or unordered (<ul>), containing list items (<li>).

Example:

<ul>
    <li>Item 1</li>
    <li>Item 2</li>
    <li>Item 3</li>
</ul>
<ol>
    <li>First Item</li>
    <li>Second Item</li>
    <li>Third Item</li>
</ol>

Tables (<table>)

Tables are used to display data in rows and columns. They consist of a combination of <table>, <tr> (table row), <td> (table data), and <th> (table header) elements.

Example:

<table>
    <tr>
        <th>Header 1</th>
        <th>Header 2</th>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>Data 1</td>
        <td>Data 2</td>
    </tr>
</table>

Divs

Divs are used to group elements together in a container. They are used to apply styles to a group of elements.

Example:

<div>
    <p>Paragraph 1</p>
    <p>Paragraph 2</p>
</div>
